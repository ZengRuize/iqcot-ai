## Diagnosis

R049G revises the R049F interpretation.  The earlier severe undervoltage was
not a valid controller result because the early-window lower bound was missing.
After repair, phase-selective Ton-min truncation is structurally effective but
not beneficial on the mild active-HS row: it trades remaining-on-time removal
for an earlier and larger local voltage spike.

The PR-ECB action model must therefore distinguish:

- immediate local spike after hard active-HS interruption;
- recovery peak several microseconds later;
- late undershoot / settling behavior.
